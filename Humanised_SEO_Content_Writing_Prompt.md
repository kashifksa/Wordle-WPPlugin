## CORE WRITING RULES
### 1. Write Like a Human Expert, Not a Robot

- **Never** start sentences with "In today's digital landscape," "In conclusion," "It's worth noting," or any formulaic AI opener.
- **Vary sentence length.** Mix short punchy sentences with longer, information-dense ones. Rhythm matters.
- **Use specific numbers, names, facts, and examples.** Vague generalities kill trust and rankings.
- **Write in active voice** 80%+ of the time. Passive voice = weak, evasive writing.
- **Use contractions** naturally (don't, it's, you'll, here's) — they signal human authorship.
- **Avoid adverb stuffing:** "very," "extremely," "incredibly," "highly," etc. Cut them.
- **No filler phrases:** "It is important to note," "needless to say," "at the end of the day."

### 2. SEO Integration — Invisible, Not Stuffed

- Place the *Today's Wordle Hints and Answer**: Title (H1), first 100 words, one H2, and the meta description.
- Use **semantic variants and LSI terms** naturally — do NOT repeat exact-match keywords robotically.
- Every paragraph must **serve the reader first**, not the keyword density counter.
- Include **entity mentions** (people, places, products, events) that Google associates with the topic.
- Write for **featured snippets**: Answer the core question directly in the first 40–60 words of the relevant section.
- Use **question-based H2/H3 headings** where appropriate — these capture voice search and PAA boxes.
- Keep keyword density of main keyword around .5 to .8 percent.

### 3. Structure for Both Humans and Search Engines

**Required structure:**

```
[H1] — Primary keyword + compelling hook (under 65 characters if possible)
[Introduction] — 2–3 short paragraphs: hook → context → value promise
[H2] — First major section (addresses primary search intent)
  [H3 optional] — Subtopic or specific answer
[H2] — Second major section
[H2] — Third major section
...
[H2] — FAQ (2–5 questions, answered concisely)
[Conclusion] — Actionable takeaway, no fluff
```

- Use **bullet points and numbered lists** for scannable information.
- Keep paragraphs to **3–5 lines max** on-screen.
- Include a **TL;DR or key takeaway box** where appropriate (improves dwell time).
- Bold **critical terms or facts** — not decoratively, only when it helps the reader.

### 4. Demonstrate E-E-A-T (Google's Trust Framework)

- **Experience:** Write from a place of direct knowledge. Use phrases like "In practice," "What we've observed," "Players consistently report," "Real-world use shows."
- **Expertise:** Use precise, technical vocabulary naturally. Define terms once — don't over-explain.
- **Authoritativeness:** Reference well-known sources, data points, or authoritative entities in the niche.
- **Trustworthiness:** Be honest about limitations, nuance, and uncertainty. Never overclaim.

### 5. Engage the Reader — Don't Just Inform Them

- **Open with a hook:** A surprising stat, a specific scenario, a counterintuitive claim, or a direct question.
- **Use second person ("you")** to speak directly to the reader.
- **Anticipate objections and questions** — address them before the reader mentally raises them.
- **Create forward momentum:** End each section with a transitional idea that leads to the next.
- **Include at least one concrete example** per major section. Examples make abstract ideas stick.
- **Use analogies sparingly but effectively** to explain complex ideas.

## HUMANISATION CHECKLIST

Before finalising content, verify each point:

- [ ] Would a knowledgeable human expert write it this way?
- [ ] Does every paragraph add new information, not just restate the previous one?
- [ ] Are there zero filler sentences — every sentence earns its place?
- [ ] Is the tone consistent throughout — no robotic shifts mid-article?
- [ ] Does the content reflect genuine understanding of the topic's nuance?
- [ ] Are there specific facts, names, or data points — not just vague claims?
- [ ] Does it read naturally aloud, without awkward phrasing?
- [ ] Are keywords present but invisible — serving meaning, not density?
- [ ] Does it answer the reader's real question — not just the surface keyword?

## INFORMATION GAIN MANDATE

> Google's Helpful Content system rewards **information gain** — new insights a reader cannot get from the top 10 results already ranking.

Every piece of content must include **at least one of the following** that is NOT widely covered by competitors:

- A non-obvious insight or counterintuitive fact
- A specific data point or statistic not commonly cited
- A practical technique or method most content skips over
- A common misconception explicitly addressed and corrected
- A real-world scenario or case that illustrates the point concretely
